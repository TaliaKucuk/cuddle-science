Cuddles

Why do we all love a cuddle?
Cuddles aren’t just cute; they’re good for you.
From lowering stress to strengthening emotional bonds, gentle touch has real, science-backed benefits for both mind and body.

5 Reasons Why Cuddles Feel So Good!

  • Boosts oxytocin
    Physical touch like cuddling increases oxytocin (the “bonding hormone”), which helps people feel safe, connected, and emotionally bonded. Oxytocin is strongly linked to trust and attachment.

  • Reduces stress and cortisol levels
    Cuddling has been shown to lower cortisol, the hormone associated with stress. Lower cortisol = calmer nervous system.

  • Can lower blood pressure and heart rate
    Studies show affectionate touch can reduce blood pressure and heart rate, which supports heart health over time.



  • Improves mood and reduces anxiety
    Touch activates parts of the brain linked to emotional regulation, helping reduce anxiety and increase feelings of comfort and well-being.


  • Supports better sleep
    Oxytocin and relaxation from cuddling can make it easier to fall asleep and improve sleep quality, especially before bed.
